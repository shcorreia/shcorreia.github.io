Wolbach Library at the Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics

I currently work at the Wolbach Library in Cambridge, Massachusetts. I am the Assistant Community Coordinator for Project PHaEDRA. Project PHaEDRA is a project to preserve the work of the Harvard Computers, which was a group of female astronomers in the late 19th-early 20th centuries. Some of these astronomers include Annie Jump Cannon, Williamina Fleming, Cecilia Payne, and Henrietta Swan Leavitt.

Millicent Library

I have worked at the Millicent Library in Fairhaven, Massachusetts from 2013-present. I started out as a page, but for the last three years I have worked at the circulation desk. I have continued to work at the Millicent Library on weekends and breaks from college. Working at the Millicent Library kindled my interest in creating a career out of working in libraries.

I feel fortunate to have started out my library career at the Millicent Library because it is a building that is full of history. The library was built in 1893 by Henry Huttleston Rogers, a leader in Standard Oil. He had the library built in memory of his daughter Millicent, who died at the age of 17. Many of my coworkers also attended Simmons University.
